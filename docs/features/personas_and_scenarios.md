# User Personas and Scenarios

## Persona 1: Lihle (Student)
- *Age*: 20
- *Occupation*: 2nd-year Computer Science student at iWS
- *Goal*: Grab lunch and get help from admin without missing lectures or standing in long queues.

### Scenario
Lihle opens the CampusQueue app during her 15-min break. She checks live queue times for the cafeteria and sees it’s 25 min long. Instead, she books a 12:30 pickup slot for a pre-ordered meal. While waiting, she also books a 14:00 appointment with Student Admin to sort out her registration issue. She gets WhatsApp confirmations for both and a reminder 10 min before each slot. No queues, no missed classes.


## Persona 2: Mr. Dlamini (Staff)
- *Age*: 45
- *Occupation*: Student Admin Officer
- *Goal*: Manage student consultations efficiently and reduce overcrowding at the office during registration.

##Scenario
Mr. Dlamini logs into the CampusQueue staff dashboard at 8am. He sees 18 appointments booked for the day, with student names, issues, and uploaded docs already attached. He sets his availability to 10am–3pm and adds a 15-min buffer between slots. When a student is late, he marks them as "no-show" and the system auto-notifies the next person that their slot moved up. By 3pm, he’s helped everyone without a chaotic hallway queue.



## Persona 3: Ms. Khumalo (Finance Admin)
- *Age*: 38  
- *Occupation*: Finance Officer at iWS, handles fee queries + payment plans  
- *Goal*: Stop students lining up for hours just to ask “how much do I still owe?” and reduce walk-ins during fee deadlines.

### Scenario  
It’s fee clearance week. Ms. Khumalo opens CampusQueue and sees her day is already mapped: 22 booked slots, each with the student number and query type pre-selected. Before the student even arrives, she pulls their statement from ITS and uploads a screenshot to the appointment. At 10:00, Thabo arrives for his slot. She scans his QR, sees he needs a payment plan, and they’re done in 8 min because she prepped. When a student cancels last-minute, the system auto-offers the slot to someone on the waitlist via WhatsApp. By 4pm she’s cleared her queue, no crowd at her door, and she exports a report showing 90% show-up rate vs last year’s chaos.
